1 Introduction

Chaffinches (Fringilla coelebs) are one of the most wide spread and abundant birds in Britian (Chaffinch, n.d.a). The average weight of the chaffinch is around 24g (Chaffinch, n.d.b) but it is unstated whether there is a difference between male and female averages. Since male and female chaffinches have such widely varying colour and plumage (see Figure 1.1) it is reasonable to assume they would differ in mass too. Thats what our analysis hopes to uncover.

Figure 1.1: Photo of a male (left) and a female (right) chaffinch showcasing their differing colour patterns. Copyright 2007, Fonzy, https://myolympus.org/document.php?id=9285

2 Methods

We measured the mass of 20 chaffinches for both sexes and used R (R Core Team 2020) along with the tidyverse package (Wickham et al. 2019) to analyse the results to see if there is a significant difference between the mass of the sexes.
